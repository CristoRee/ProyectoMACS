Client Manual — BinaryTEC

Welcome guide to managing your repair requests.

1. Introduction

Welcome to BinaryTEC. This guide will show you how to create an account, request a repair for your devices, and how to track the progress of your ticket until it is resolved.

2. Creating Your Account and Logging In

To use our services, you first need an account.

Register a New Account

In the upper right corner of the homepage, click the user icon and select "Register".
Complete the form with your username, email, and a secure password.
Confirm your password and click "Register User".

Sign In

Click the user icon and select "Sign In".
Enter your username and password.
Click "Enter". If your details are correct, you will access your account.

3. Managing Your Requests

Once inside your account, the process is very simple.

Step 1: Request a Repair

When you have a device with issues, this is the first step:

On the homepage, click the large "Request Repair" button.
Complete the form with your device details (e.g., Laptop, HP, Pavilion 15).
Describe the problem you are experiencing (e.g., "The screen flickers," "It won't turn on").
(Optional) If you have photos of the problem, you can attach them to help the technician.
Click "Submit Request".
Important: If you are not logged in, the "Request Repair" button will take you to the login page first.

Step 2: View Your Requests

To see the status of all your tickets, click on your profile picture (top right) and select "My Requests", or use the shortcut button on the homepage.

On this screen, you will find two lists:

  • Active Requests: Tickets that are currently in process (Submitted, In diagnosis, etc.).
  • Completed Requests: A history of all jobs that have already been completed.

Step 3: Understanding Your Ticket Status

In the request list, you will see a colored badge that indicates the status of your repair. The most common statuses are:

  • Submitted: We have received your request and it will be assigned soon.
  • In Diagnosis: A technician is reviewing your device.
  • Awaiting Approval: The technician has sent you a quote or question and is waiting for your response.
  • In Repair: We are working on the solution!
  • Ready for Pickup: Your device is repaired and you can come get it.

Step 4: Ticket Actions (Chat and Delete)

In the "Actions" column of your active requests, you have two options:

Chat with Technician

This button allows you to talk directly with the technician assigned to your case. Please note:

  • The chat button will appear **gray and disabled** at first.
  • It will turn **blue and become functional** the moment a technician is assigned to your ticket.

Delete Request

If you wish to cancel a request you just created or no longer need, you can use this button. The system will ask for confirmation before deleting it.

4. Your Account and Preferences

You have full control over your account and the platform's appearance.

My Profile

By clicking your profile picture and selecting "My Profile", you can:

  • Change your Profile Picture: Upload a new image from your device.
  • Update your Details: Change your username, email, or phone number.
  • Change your Password: You will be asked for your current password for security.
  • Activate Dark Mode: Use the toggle to switch to a dark visual theme, which is more comfortable for your eyes in low-light conditions. The system will remember your choice.

Email Notifications

You don't need to do anything to activate them. When your ticket reaches an important status (like "Ready for Pickup"), our system will automatically send a **notification to your email** to keep you informed.
